## Technical taste

- TypeScript is his strongest and favorite language.
- Preferred frontend ecosystem: TanStack.
- Preferred backend frameworks: NestJS or FastAPI.
- Preferred database: PostgreSQL.
- For a small product, he likes a minimal backend framework that can also serve the frontend, such as FastAPI.
- TypeScript is practical and useful. React and Next.js are badly designed in places, but remain necessary and important. Python is extremely useful but can be verbose. Java feels old and is not something he enjoys using.
- An abstraction is good when it is genuinely necessary. Duplication is better when the abstraction would be overkill.
- He approaches database design carefully for large systems and moves faster for small ones.
- Automated tests matter, but he values tests thoughtfully designed by humans most.
- Accessibility and security are always important. Performance, observability, and production-debugging infrastructure should be added when the product actually needs them.
- He dislikes microservices unless the scale or domain genuinely calls for them.
- He is not generally a fan of serverless systems, although they can be a lifesaver in the right situation.
- He adds a dependency when reproducing the same functionality would not be quick and safe.
- He buys a service when a capability would take too long to build, is highly nuanced, or would be difficult to maintain responsibly.
- Clean code is maintainable code that both people and AI coding tools can continue building on safely.
- He pushes back on over-abstraction, over-engineering, and perfectionism.
- He used to reach for the fanciest stack, such as Next.js, for almost everything. Now he believes every stack has its own purpose and place.
